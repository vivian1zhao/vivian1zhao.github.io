About me

My name is Vivian Zhao.

Who I am: I’m a senior at Rutgers University double majoring in Data Science and Computer Science. Much of my work centers on applied analytics and interpretable machine learning, and I’ve had the chance to explore that through roles that all tie back to one theme: using data to make complex things clearer and more human-centered.

What excites me: Turning messy, ambiguous data problems into clean, meaningful insights. I love figuring out why something isn’t working, building models or systems that actually help people, and approaching problems with both analytical and empathetic thinking. Collaboration, asking better questions, and clear communication shape how I work.

A little more about me: I’m someone who learns quickly and loves learning broadly, whether it’s about music, design, psychology, photography, or the ways technology shapes everyday life. I enjoy exploring new perspectives, picking up creative hobbies, and finding the intersection between the analytical and the artistic.
